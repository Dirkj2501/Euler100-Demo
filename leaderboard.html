📐 Euler Index (EI) — How Rankings Are Calculated

▼

The Euler Index (EI) is a composite score from 0 to 1000 designed to reward genuine mathematical mastery. It has three components that together ensure rankings reflect both breadth of achievement and depth of understanding.

EULER INDEX FORMULA
EI = J + P + R    (max 1000)
J = Journey Score (max 500)  |  P = Precision Score (max 350)  |  R = Resilience Score (max 150)
🗺️
Journey Score (J)
max 500 pts
J = (Tests Passed ÷ 49) × 500

Measures progress through all 7 stages. Each stage has 7 tests of 10 questions. You must score 7/10 or above to pass a test and unlock the next one.
🎯
Precision Score (P)
max 350 pts
P = First-Attempt Accuracy × 350

The share of all questions you answered correctly on your very first attempt. Rewards true mastery — knowing the answer, not learning it under exam pressure.
💪
Resilience Score (R)
max 150 pts
R = Second-Attempt Recovery Rate × 150

Of the questions you got wrong first time, how many did you get right on your second attempt? Rewards learning from mistakes during the assessment.
📊 Worked Example

Suppose a candidate has completed 35 tests (all of stages 1–5), with 85% first-attempt accuracy and 70% second-attempt recovery:

J = (35 ÷ 49) × 500 = 357
P = 0.85 × 350 = 297
R = 0.70 × 150 = 105
EI = 357 + 297 + 105 = 759

Note: A candidate at a lower stage with exceptional accuracy can outrank a candidate at a higher stage with poor precision. This is intentional — the EI rewards mastery, not just advancement speed.

Stage Difficulty Standards

Each stage's question difficulty is calibrated against internationally recognised junior mathematics competition standards:

Stage Level Name Tier Tests Equivalent Competition Standard Key Topics
1 🧭 Explorer Pre-Academy 7 × 10 Q AMC 8 / UKMT Junior Mathematical Challenge (foundations) Arithmetic, fractions, basic algebra, 2D geometry, ratios, percentages, speed
2 ⛰️ Adventurer Pre-Academy 7 × 10 Q High-end AMC 8 / UKMT JMC (upper range) HCF/LCM, expanding brackets, probability, coordinate geometry, data handling
3 🧗 Alpinist Pre-Academy 7 × 10 Q UKMT JMC top band / Low AMC 10 (transition to proof thinking) Simultaneous equations, quadratics, 3D geometry, Pythagoras, sequences, intro proof
4 ✈️ Aviator Euler Academy 7 × 10 Q AMC 10 / UKMT Junior Mathematical Olympiad Advanced algebra, trigonometry, combinatorics intro, modular arithmetic, inequalities
5 🚀 Astronaut Euler Academy 7 × 10 Q AMC 12 / AIME foundation / UKMT Intermediate Olympiad Polynomial theory, advanced combinatorics, series, competition geometry, expected value
6 ♟️ Grandmaster Euler Academy 7 × 10 Q AIME / USA(J)MO (national elite level) Functional equations, advanced number theory, olympiad geometry, proof writing
7 ⭐ Genius Euler Academy 7 × 10 Q IMO Shortlist / IMO level (world-class) Diophantine equations, projective geometry, advanced combinatorics, open proof problems

🛡️ Integrity & Anti-Gaming Provisions

One Account Per Candidate

Registration requires verified parent or teacher consent. Duplicate accounts are detected through cross-referencing of consent records and permanently disqualified.

Randomised Question Pools

Each stage contains a large pool of questions. Tests draw randomly and present questions in random order — memorising answers from previous attempts provides no advantage.

Response Pattern Analysis

STELLA AI monitors session timing and answer patterns. Unusually fast correct responses, irregular switching between attempts, and other anomalies are flagged for human review.

Mastery Cannot Be Faked

The Precision Score (P) component rewards genuine first-attempt knowledge. Racing through stages with poor accuracy yields a low EI — only true understanding produces a high ranking.

School & Teacher Accountability

Candidates enrolled via teacher class code are accountable through their school. Teachers are notified of any integrity concerns and may be required to supervise assessments.

Disentis Finale Verification

All Top 100 finalists must pass an in-person verification assessment at Kloster Disentis. Only candidates who can demonstrate their Euler Index performance live are confirmed as finalists.

The Euler Index formula, stage difficulty calibration, and integrity provisions are reviewed annually by the Euler100 Academic Committee. Full methodology documentation is available in the Euler100 Terms & Conditions. Competition standards are benchmarked against AMC (Mathematical Association of America), UKMT (United Kingdom Mathematics Trust), and IMO (International Mathematical Olympiad) public guidelines.
